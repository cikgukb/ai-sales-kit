1
Title
The hook — captures attention in under 2 seconds
Wording
Short, punchy, benefit-driven. 3–7 words max. No jargon.
Typography
Bold, high-contrast typeface. Largest element on the poster.
Placement
Top third or center. Never buried below the fold.
Tone
Match brand voice: aspirational, urgent, emotional, or informational.
💡 Specify: the exact headline text, the emotion it should trigger (e.g. excitement, curiosity, trust), and target audience.
2
Main image
The anchor — communicates value at a glance
Subject
Product, person, lifestyle scene, or abstract visual metaphor.
Style
Photorealistic, illustrated, flat design, 3D render, or collage.
Composition
Rule of thirds, centred hero, or full bleed. Leave breathing room for text.
Colour palette
2–4 dominant colours. Match or contrast brand colours intentionally.
Mood / lighting
Bright & clean, dramatic & dark, warm & inviting, cool & minimal.
Background
Solid, gradient, bokeh, texture, or transparent. Ensure title readability.
💡 Specify: what the image depicts, the visual style, mood, colours, and whether the product/person should be prominent or secondary.
3
Call to action
The closer — tells the viewer exactly what to do next
CTA text
Action verb + clear outcome. e.g. "Shop now", "Book a free call", "Download today".
Visual treatment
Button shape, bold text block, or badge. Must stand out from the main image.
Placement
Bottom third is standard. Near the product if layout allows.
Supporting info
Price, deadline, URL, QR code, or offer details placed close to CTA.
💡 Specify: the exact CTA phrase, urgency level (e.g. limited offer vs evergreen), and any supporting detail like a price or date.
